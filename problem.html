Problem to solve

Problem — is a huge waste of time and money while building and supporting uninstrumented software full of elements, options and features.

If you're not instrumenting your stuff, you don't deserve any investment let me to say that. You're in a big trouble, if you not. You're flying blind...
Marty Cagan at Heavybit Conference, 2013
Now we've got a product that it takes forever to do anything with: it's hard to learn, it's hard to use, it's hard to maintain. Just making a small change takes forever because of so many options and configuration changes we need to test with any possible combination...
Jared Spool at MTP Conference, 2015

Below is a 1 minute YouTube clip with Jared Spool speaking at Mind The Product Conference, San Francisco, 2015, explaining eXperience rot, ways to overcome it and why it matters to overcome:

Software Development Teams burn so much time and money on designing, building, testing, delivery, support of their apps and services and yet they don't know: (1) which elements and features, (2) when, (3) how much and (4) by whom of their users, customers and players are being used.

Why to keep something on interface (and spend resources on it's support) if no one actually using it? Why not to change barely used element with much more wanted one insted to increase satisfaction from UX/CX/PX? Why not to talk about your findings to your customers and not to ask them: why do you use this feature so much or why don't you use it at all?

I'll tell you why: because teams don't have that data because they didn't instrument their apps.

But imagine how things could change if they instrumented their apps and got that data?

Example

On a simple UI screen below there are at least 16 unique interactive web-elements (links, buttons, checkboxes, input fields, etc.):

Do we know: (1) who of users uses (2) which of those web elements, (3) when they used them and (4) how much? Which elements are not being used at all — so we could get rid of them? We don't know.

But what if we had such web elements usage analytics for that page:

Thanks to this Product UX/CX/PX Analytics tool we can see that:

  • "New Ticket" button is the most used UI element;
  • "Select item" checkbox is the least used UI element;
  • This data is among all categories of users: Visitor, Free trial and Paid;
  • This data if only for the last 5 minutes;

And we can filter by:

  • User ID(s) — to see what any particular user does;
  • User Group ID(s) — to see what particular group of users (for example: Paid, Managers) does;
  • Element — to see who and when and how much used an element;
  • Page / Screen — to analyse and optimize every single page;
  • Time — to limit all of above by time frame.

Now we have the data and we can start making data-driven UX/CX/PX decisions for our Digital Products. Now we can filter what our "Paid" users use and optimize UX of the app for them. We also can filter for "Free trial" and optimize UX for them. And so on...
